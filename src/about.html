Our Story

How we began and where we're going

Therapy Council was founded in 2018 by a group of dedicated mental health professionals who shared a vision: to create a therapy practice that combines evidence-based techniques with genuine compassion and accessibility. We recognized that many people face barriers to receiving quality mental health care, whether due to cost, stigma, or lack of tailored approaches.

What began as a small practice has grown into a diverse team of specialized therapists, each bringing unique skills and perspectives to our collaborative approach. Throughout our growth, we've remained committed to our founding principles: providing personalized care, creating a safe and inclusive environment, and empowering our clients to achieve meaningful change in their lives.

Today, Therapy Council serves hundreds of individuals, couples, and families across our community. We continue to evolve our practices based on the latest research, client feedback, and emerging therapeutic approaches, ensuring that we provide the most effective care possible.

Our Therapeutic Approach

How we work with clients to create positive change

At Therapy Council, we take an integrative approach to therapy, drawing from multiple evidence-based methods to address each client's unique needs. We believe that effective therapy combines proven techniques with a strong therapeutic relationship, creating a foundation for meaningful and lasting change.

Our therapists are trained in a variety of modalities, including:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Helps identify and change thought patterns that negatively influence behavior and emotions.
  • Mindfulness-Based Approaches: Cultivates present-moment awareness to reduce stress and enhance well-being.
  • Emotion-Focused Therapy: Works with emotions as a primary source of meaning and direction for positive change.
  • Solution-Focused Brief Therapy: Focuses on solutions rather than problems by identifying resources and future hopes.
  • Psychodynamic Therapy: Explores unconscious processes and past experiences that influence current behavior.
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT): Helps clients accept difficult thoughts and feelings while committing to behavior change.

Rather than adhering strictly to one methodology, we customize our approach based on what research and experience indicate will be most effective for your specific situation. Throughout the therapy process, we regularly check in about what's working and what isn't, adjusting our methods as needed to ensure you're progressing toward your goals.

We believe that therapy should be both supportive and challenging, providing a compassionate space for exploration while also encouraging growth and positive change. Our goal is to help you not only resolve immediate concerns but also develop the insights and skills that will serve you well beyond your time in therapy.
